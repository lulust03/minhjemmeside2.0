When was the first Nakba?

May 15 1948

Nakba

During the foundational events of the Nakba, approximately half of Palestine's predominantly Arab population, or around 750,000 people, were expelled from their homes or made to flee through various violent means.

The six day war

The Six-Day War resulted in Israel's occupation of the West Bank, Gaza Strip, and East Jerusalem, significantly impacting the Palestinian population. Following the war, approximately 300,000 Palestinians were displaced, exacerbating the refugee crisis.

What restrictions do Palestinians face in times like these?

Palestinians face severe restrictions on movement, including checkpoints and barriers that limit their access for medical care, food shortages, and lack of clean water.

What can we do to support Palestinian rights and raise awareness?

Raise awareness by educating ourselves and others, sharing credible information on social media, and participating in protests. Supporting humanitarian organizations through donations and advocating for policy changes by contacting representatives also helps.
